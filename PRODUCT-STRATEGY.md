# Cake App Product Strategy

## Core Principle
One app, one main flow, designed to work well for different real customer situations.

We are not building separate apps for separate personas.
We are building one clear ordering experience that adapts through smart defaults, shortcuts, and progressive depth.

## Main Customer Groups

### 1. Busy professionals
- Usually ordering quickly from work, commute, or between tasks
- Need speed, confidence, and minimal decisions
- Care about clear timing, saved details, and fast reorder

### 2. Partners and family planners
- Ordering for birthdays, anniversaries, kids events, and family moments
- Need confidence they are choosing the right cake
- Care about occasion guidance, customization, and reliability

### 3. Younger buyers
- Lower budget, mobile-first, faster decisions
- Often want simple cakes with less customization friction
- Care about speed, affordability, and easy checkout

### 4. Event buyers
- Ordering for weddings, baby showers, office events, and larger gatherings
- Need more confidence, more detail, and clearer coordination
- Care about timing, order accuracy, and customization depth

### 5. Repeat family customers
- Already know what they like
- Want saved cakes, saved addresses, fast reorder, and less repetition
- Care about convenience and consistency

## Shared Customer Needs
Across all groups, customers mainly want:
- not to mess up the order
- confidence the cake will look right
- clear pickup or delivery timing
- easy saved addresses and cards
- fast reorder
- fewer decisions when rushed
- a strong confirmation moment after payment

## Product Direction

### 1. Occasion-first entry
Lead with the reason for ordering, not a blank customization screen.
Examples:
- Birthday
- Anniversary
- Kids party
- Baby shower
- Wedding
- Office event
- Just because

This reduces stress and helps users feel guided.

### 2. Simple-first, advanced-second
Default to the fastest useful path.
Then allow deeper customization for customers who want it.

That means:
- simple presets first
- advanced cake builder available after
- clear edit path without forcing complexity on everyone

### 3. Pickup and delivery as equal fulfillment modes
The app should support both naturally.

Rules:
- both include subtotal + service fee + GST/HST
- delivery adds delivery fee
- pickup does not

UX:
- fulfillment selector early in checkout
- pickup gets time-slot confidence
- delivery gets address confidence and route-style confirmation

### 4. Strong account memory
The app should remember useful things so repeat orders get easier over time.

That includes:
- saved addresses
- saved cards
- saved cakes
- past orders
- quick reorder
- preferred bakery/location

### 5. Confidence-driven confirmation
After payment, the app should reduce anxiety.

Confirmation should clearly show:
- order ID
- total paid
- pickup window or delivery address
- card used
- fulfillment type
- route/progress feel for delivery

## UX Model for One App
We are not branching into different products by persona.
Instead, one app serves everyone through:
- better defaults
- occasion shortcuts
- saved data
- reorder paths
- progressive disclosure

### Example
A busy professional sees:
- quick preset
- saved address
- saved card
- fast checkout

An event buyer sees:
- same app
- same core flow
- more customization options
- more detailed review before payment

That is one system, not separate apps.

## Recommended Flow Structure
1. Choose occasion
2. Choose or browse cake direction
3. Customize if needed
4. Add to cart
5. Choose pickup or delivery
6. Review totals clearly
7. Login before payment
8. Pay with saved or new card
9. See strong confirmation screen
10. Reorder later from account

## Feature Priorities

### Highest priority
- occasion-first entry
- pickup + delivery support
- saved addresses
- saved cards
- order history
- reorder flow
- strong confirmation screen

### Next priority
- presets by occasion
- repeat-order shortcuts
- better trust cues around cake quality and timing
- improved event-order review flow

### Later priority
- team/collaborative ordering for events
- scheduled reminders for recurring family events
- smarter recommendations from prior orders

## Product Standard
Every screen should answer:
- what is this user trying to do right now?
- what are they worried about?
- how do we help them finish faster with more confidence?

That is the lens.
Not “how do we copy Uber exactly?”

## Bottom Line
Build one cake-ordering app that feels:
- fast for rushed users
- safe for nervous users
- flexible for custom users
- effortless for repeat users

That is the strategy.
